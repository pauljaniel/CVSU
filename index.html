HISTORY



Cavite State University (CvSU) began in 1906 as the Indang Intermediate School, with American Thomasites serving as the initial teachers. The school's name changed several times, including Indang Farm School in 1918, Indang Rural High School in 1927, and Don Severino National Agriculture School in 1958. Don Severino Agricultural College (DSAC) was established in 1964 when Republic Act 3917 turned the school into a state college.


DSAC was renamed Cavite State University on January 22, 1998, under Republic Act No. 8468. By virtue of CHED Memo No. 27, the Cavite College of Fisheries (CACOF) in Naic, Cavite, and the Cavite College of Arts and Trade (CCAT) in Rosario, Cavite, were merged into the University in 2001. 2000. Since then, more campuses across the province have been formed through memoranda of arrangement with the LGUs. CvSU now offers 11 campuses in the province. The main campus, titled Don Severino delas Alas Campus, spans around 70 hectares and is located in Indang, Cavite.


CvSU's mission is "to provide excellent, equitable, and relevant educational opportunities in the arts, sciences, and technology through quality instruction and responsive research and development activities." It will generate professional, competent, and morally upright persons to ensure global competitiveness."
